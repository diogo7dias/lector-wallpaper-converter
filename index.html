Drop images here or click to choose PNG · JPG · WebP · GIF · BMP · PXC — pick as many as you like. Drop an existing .pxc to re-target it to the other screen.
How it works & the auto settings

PXC is the device's packed format: a 4-byte little-endian width,height header then 2 bits/pixel (4 gray levels: black · dark · light · white), MSB-first. BMP output is a standard 2-bit, 4-gray bitmap the reader also accepts.

  • Auto rendering — the image's tonal range is measured. Photographs (many tones) get Floyd–Steinberg dithering for smooth gradients; logos / line art / text (few tones) get crisp thresholding with no dither. Click the dither / threshold badge under any result to flip that one image; changing the Rendering dropdown resets them all.
  • Auto-contrast — stretches the tones to use all four levels, so flat or low-contrast images don't come out muddy. Turn off to keep exact tones.
  • Fit — Cover fills the screen and crops the overflow (best for most wallpapers); Fit letterboxes the whole image; Stretch distorts to fill.
  • Invert — only if a wallpaper shows up looking like a photo negative on your panel; leave off for the X4 build.
  • Re-target an existing wallpaper — drop a .pxc straight in. It's decoded and previewed, and the card shows which reader it was made for (from X3 / from X4, or its raw size if neither). Switch Screen to the other reader and it re-fits and re-encodes for it (the badge then reads e.g. X3 → X4).

All conversion is local (HTML canvas). No server, no upload.
